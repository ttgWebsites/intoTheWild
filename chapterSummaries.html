Chapter Summaries

  • Chapters 1 - 3
  • Alex McCandless who the novel is centered around, is seen hitchhiking around Fairbanks, Alaska by Jim Gallien
  • Gallien asks Alex where he his headed and Alex tells of his plans to travel to Denali where he will hike and live in the wilderness for a few months
  • Gallien is initially worried by Alex's plans as Alex seems to have packed very light for a trip of that extent
  • Gallien tries to dissuade Alex but ultimately can not, so he instead offers Alex his boots and some food to help on Alex's journey
  • Alex plans to take a rather unknown path north of Mt. McKinley where he will camp for a few months, Gallien argues that the hunting will not be good out that far and that Alex will have a very difficult time providing for himself, but Alex will not budge
  • Gallien takes Alex to the beginning of the trail, and during their car ride he learns that Alex has left home and spoke to none of his family or friends for the last two years and they are completely unaware of his journey
  • On April 28th, 1992, McCandless is left by Gallien at the trail, and begins his journey down the Stampede Trail
  • The Stampede Trail had been used by miners decades earlier, in the 1960's plans had been made to work on the trail, yet were abandoned after a short while. The only remnants from the work on the trail is a bus that had been used to house some of the workers
  • In September of 1992, 5 months after McCandless expedition into the Yukon, 3 moose hunters plan to take a trip out to the bus
  • Upon arrival at the bus the hunters notice a couple standing near it obviously disturbed
  • One of the hunters decides to take a look inside the bus after noticing a letter on the outside
  • The letter, written by McCandless, states that he is very sick and could use assistance
  • When one of the hunters takes a look inside he notices that McCandless is dead, lying in a sleeping bag
  • Due to a lack of a vehicle capable of transporting McCandless body back, it is left to the state troopers who collect his body the next day and with evidence gathered from the bus McCandless is transported to Anchorage for an autopsy and identification purposes
  • McCandless had no identification at the time of his death, and therefore the police are unaware to begin when it comes to locating his family
  • The novel flashes back to an earlier time in McCandless' life
  • This time McCandless' is picked up hitchhiking in South Dakota by Wayne Westerberg, a grain elevator operator
  • McCandless works for Westerberg for a short period of time until Westerberg is arrested and jailed
  • After their is no more work for McCandless in South Dakota, he travels on, however he still keeps in touch with Westerberg all the way up until his trip into the wilderness
  • We learn of McCandless background before he began his expedition across the continent to Alaska
  • McCandless was born and raised in a suburb of Washington, D.C. where he lived with his father Walt, mother Billie, and sister Carine
  • Following high school McCandless attended and graduated from Emory University, and to his parents understanding he was planning on attending Law School
  • McCandless tells his family he will spend the summer traveling before returning to school, that is the last communication he will have with them as he moves out of his apartment, and driven away in his old Datsun with all his belongings and a new name, Alex Supertramp
  • Chapters 4 - 5
  • It is now October 1990 in the story and McCandless has driven all the way to the Mojave Desert
  • While at a National Park in the desert, McCandless takes his car off road and against park rules and sets up camp
  • A flash flood strikes his camp preventing his car from starting and nearly ruining all of his campsite
  • He abandons his car as he can not get it to work again and continues on
  • He spends the next few months traveling around out west living the life of a vagabond
  • McCandless travels to Oregon where he meets a pair of rubber tramps while he is picking berries
  • He decides to travel with them for a short time but eventually leaves on his own and is given a ticket for hitchhiking on his future travels
  • He gives his address in Virginia to the officer, and his parents finally have a lead about where he might be
  • They hire a private investigator and the investigator learns that McCandless had donated his entire savings to charity shortly before his disappearance
  • The story jumps back to McCandless in South Dakota, after he is finished working their he travels south to Arizona where he buys a canoe and plans to travel down the Colorado River until he reaches the Gulf of California
  • After a long canoe journey that causes him to get lost in a series of canals he makes it to the gulf with the help of some hunters he finally reaches the gulf
  • He travels along the coast for a while, but after nearly being washed out to sea, he decides to give up the canoe and head north again, it is now January, 1991
  • Traveling from Mexico, he tries to sneak back into the US but is caught by immigration who hassle him but ultimately let him go
  • He settles down for a short time in Los Angeles where he gets an I.D. and finds a job, but ultimately decides he can not stand society and moves on
  • After brief stops in Las Vegas and Oregon he settle in Bullhead City, Arizona for quite a while where he gets a job at a McDonald's using his real name and social security number
  • After quitting his job at McDonald's he returns north where he meets up with Jan Burress and Bob, the two rubber tramps he met earlier
  • He works for them at a flea market, and gets along well with them as they become very fond of him
  • He often talks of his dream to go to Alaska and asks Bob for survival tips
  • He leaves again, he refuses money and clothing from Jan
  • Chapters 6 - 9
  • McCandless travels to the Anza-Borrego Desert State Park where he sets up camp
  • While living their he comes across an elderly man, Ronald Franz, who gives him a ride
  • The two become friends, and Franz, thinking McCandless is just some uneducated bum, tries to reform him
  • They spend a lot of time together and become very close, but it is ultimately time for McCandless to move on again, and Franz agrees to drive him to Colorado so that he can continue on back to South Dakota from there
  • After dropping off McCandless, Franz is very sad because he has lost his only friend, McCandless writes him a letter and encourages him to live his life similar to himself, and Franz takes him up on that
  • Franz travels the country and waits for McCandless' return from Alaska, however he learns of his death from a pair of hitchhikers, and is very upset. Franz renounces his faith, and returns to drinking, something he had given up long ago
  • McCandless returns to work for Westerberg, this time he strikes a friendship with Westerberg's girlfriend, and they become very close
  • Neither Westerberg or his girlfriend, ask McCandless about his family, but the reader does learn a bit about McCandless' background
  • McCandless' father Walt was a very stubborn and controlling man causing McCandless to resent him, he planned to cut all relations with his parents, and he had informed his sister Carine of this
  • McCandless leaves South Dakota again for his journey to Alaska, he vows to return the next fall to work again
  • The author then tells of how when he first wrote an article about McCandless in Outside magazine he received a lot of criticism as many Alaskans thought McCandless didn't respect the wilderness
  • McCandless' story becomes infamous along with other similar stories, a man Gene Rosellini traveled into the Alaskan wilderness and lived for ten years on his own with only supplies he could make himself
  • McCandless also drew comparison to a young man John Waterman, who had a rocky family relationship that lead to rash climbing decisions, one a very dangerous climb of Denali where he was believed to have been killed, another man Car McCunn, a photographer, flew out into the wilderness where he planned to take photos. He however forgot to plan a return flight, and ultimately died in the winter
  • McCandless also draws comparison to Everett Ruess, he lived many decades before McCandless yet had a very similar story of hitchhiking and traveling across the US before his believed deat
  • Chapters 10 - 11
  • Following McCandless' death, police in Anchorage try to locate his family
  • They first publish an article which is read by Jim Gallien who informs the police about his run in with McCandless
  • Gallien tells Police that he believes McCandless is from South Dakota, which is where they look next
  • Though McCandless had lied to Gallien about his origins, Westerberg learns about McCandless death and informs authorities that he had once worked for him
  • Through work statements it is learned that McCandless was from Virginia and authorities try to reach his family by looking their
  • McCandless' family had moved from Virginia, but his half brother still lived their
  • After hearing about a hiker from Virginia that died and looking at the pictures, Sam, McCandless' half-brother, realizes that it was McCandless
  • Sam travels to Maryland to tell Walt and Billie that McCandless has died
  • Weeks later the author, Jon Krakauer, travels to speak with Walt and Billie
  • Walt has seemed to mellow since McCandless' disappearance
  • During their conversation, Krakauer learns that Chris McCandless had grown up a very gifted and social child
  • He was liked by almost everybody, and very social, yet he was also content just being alone as well
  • Krakauer learns that as money became tight in the McCandless household, Billie and Walt began to fight occasionally, which seemed to have a profound effect on Chris
  • It seems apparent throughout Chris childhood that he is very talented in many different things, he received almost all A's, and was very active in his community as well as in helping fight racial oppression in South Africa
  • His family recognized his desire to be different, especially in high school
  • He stated that he did not want to attend college or see it necessary, and he was also ashamed at his parents wealth as he believed wealth was inherently evil
  • Chris' apparent issues with wealth helps explain his desire to part from all his belongings and head out on his journey
  • Chapters 12 - 13
  • After McCandless' graduation from high school his parents threw him a large party, at the party he gives his father an expensive telescope, and provides a moving speech
  • Shortly after he leaves on a trip across country for the summer before he attends college
  • His trip gets him lost in the Mojave Desert and he nearly dies from starvation
  • McCandless returns home before school thirty pounds lighter and looking rather gruff
  • However one he attends Emory for a while, McCandless seems to settle in and enjoy it, becoming very absorbed in school and seeming to leave his vagabond ways behind
  • However he does seem to become somewhat socially introverted
  • We learn that McCandless' angst towards his father stems from something learned on his summer journey
  • His father had apparently kept a relationship going in secret with his first wife even after marrying Billie and fathering Chris
  • He broods over what he has learned and he begins to resent society in general, often complaining about all the rich and entitled kids at Emory
  • Chris becomes more and more anti social and focused on his studies throughout his time at Emory
  • He writes many passionate opinion articles, and seems to cast all his friends aside in his angst
  • Following his Junior year he again travels the US, going to Guatemala, and Fairbanks, Alaska
  • He tells his parents he will be traveling again after his graduation, and that he will visit them before the trip, however he never visits, and that is the last they see or here of him
  • His mother Billie tells of one night in July, 1992 she woke up and though she could here Chris calling to her, this was about the time that Chris died
  • Chris' sister had a rocky relationship with her parents after his disappearance however they managed to salvage their relationship sometime between his difference and death
  • Chapters 14 - 15
  • Some details about the author are given in chapter 14 and 15, Krakauer was a rather adventurous young man, with a passion for rock climbing
  • At the age of 23 Krakauer planned and attempted a very dangerous climb that could possibly take his life, and he planned to do it alone
  • The Devil's Thumb is what he plans to climb and he must travel to Alaska to do so
  • Krakauer quits his job, drives to Alaska, and then takes a boat to St. Petersburg, Alaska where he plans to set up for the climb
  • Krakauer begins the climb and the first two days seem to go alright, however on the third day their is heavy snowfall nearly blinding Krakauer and almost causing him to fall to his death
  • Krakauer struggles to make any progress over the next few days due to bad storms and dwindling supplies
  • The reader learns about Krakauer's angst towards his father much like McCandless'
  • Krakauer struggled to gain his dads approval and was constantly trying to prove him wrong, that's why he feels he has to complete the climb
  • Eventually the weather clears up enough for him to make his climb, and Krakauer finally completes the climb
  • Krakauer eventually returns back Colorado where his previous job was and begins working again
  • Chapter 16
  • The story then shifts back to McCandless, he has left South Dakota and began his journey to Alaska
  • McCandless arrives at the beginning of the Alaska Highway which is 1,500 miles from Fairbanks
  • He manages to hitchhike for a couple hundred miles before stopping and going to a group of thermal pools off the highway
  • Following his time their he tries to find another ride but has no luck for a few days
  • While at the pools McCandless meets Gaylord Stuckey, who is driving an RV to Alaska to be sold
  • Stuckey is forbidden to pick up hitchhikers, however he takes a liking to McCandless and agrees to give him a ride
  • On the ride the two men have long conversations, and McCandless begins to tell of his past, and also his plans for when he gets to Alaska
  • Stuckey warns that it is two early in the year for McCandless' journey but he is stubborn
  • McCandless arrives in Fairbanks and goes to the University where he finds a book on edible plants, he then buys some supplies for his expedition, including a gun and shells
  • McCandless sends two postcards, one to Jan, and the other to Wayne, and then heads out west
  • While walking Jim Gallien comes across him, and picks him up to give him a ride
  • Gallien drops him off at the beginning of the trail, and their is still over a foot of snow on the ground as McCandless begins his trek into the wild
  • McCandless believes that his 10 pound bag of rice and his knowledge of plants and fish will be able to keep him alive
  • He also brings with him a few books, he decides to use blank pages in his books to keep a journal
  • He travels for two days and reaches the Teklanika river, he crosses it as it is rather easy due to most of it being frozen over
  • He is finally alone, free from society, in the wild
  • Across the river he comes across an abandon bus, and uses it as shelter
  • However he is faced with reality early on, as he struggles to find food and loses a lot of weight
  • May rolls around and the weather finally changes, he manages to improve his hunting, and is finally living somewhat well in the wild
  • One day he comes across a moose and kills it
  • He hopes to use it all for food, however due to a mistake he is only able to get a few pounds of meat out of it, and the rest is left to wolves
  • McCandless decides to head back to the road, he packs all his stuff, but when he reaches the river he realizes it is way to strong to cross
  • He decides to wait it out until it is safer, however his problems are only just beginning
  • Chapters 17, 18, and the Epilogue
  • Krakauer has traveled to the river near McCandless' camp site where he died, this is about a year after his death
  • Krakauer and the men that have made the trip with him discover that only half a mile from where McCandless died is a wire with an attached basket that spans the river
  • This could have saved McCandless life, however with his desire to disconnect himself with the outside world, he refused to bring a map that would have told him this
  • The men travel across the river and make their way towards the bus where McCandless camped
  • They notice many bones of small game, which McCandless had hunted and eaten, outside the bus. They then head inside and bus where some of McCandless possessions still remain
  • Krakauer remarks on the graffiti inside the bus, McCandless being the longest and most proficient
  • The men then leave the bus and have a discussion about why McCandless is hated so much for having died in the wilderness, as it seems many people blame his death on his arrogance and ignorance
  • McCandless is compared to Sir John Franklin, a British officer in the navy in the 1800's
  • Franklin led an expedition through the wilderness of Canada, many of his men die on this journey as he was ill-prepared
  • Franklin and some men do manage to make it out alive, and Franklin is promoted and allowed to lead another expedition
  • The second expedition he leads has 128 men, all of which die due to Franklin's lack of preparation for the journey
  • Krakauer and his men agree that McCandless was not arrogant and ignorant, he did manage to survive 16 weeks in the wilderness with few tools and food, and he was aware of the risks of his journey
  • They also agree that they will never fully understand him
  • The novel returns back to McCandless, it is July and he McCandless has been writing journals
  • He mentions that he is losing weight however he is still managing to find food to eat
  • McCandless reads the book Doctor Zhivago and makes some notes in it mentioning that he is perhaps ready to return back to life in society, maybe even finally end his avoidance of intimacy
  • At the end of July their is a sudden shift in McCandless' journal writings
  • He mentions that he has become severely weak and is having trouble standing up and living normally
  • Through the readings of his journals, Krakauer learns that the most likely cause of the deterioration of McCandless health is a poisonous seed
  • McCandless had been eating wild potato's for quite a while however they became too tough to eat, he then instead switched to eating the seeds from the plant, however he believed he most likely misinterpreted seeds from a different poisonous plant as safe to eat
  • Krakauer sends the seeds to be tested for poison, yet the tests prove positive, Krakauer then comes to believe that it is mold on the seeds that caused his sudden bad health
  • Over the next few days McCandless struggles greatly, he hunts small game, but gains no nourishment from food do to the poison in his body
  • He uses this page to write a farewell message “I have had a happy life and thank the Lord. Goodbye and may God bless all!"
  • He then heads outside the bus and takes a final picture of himself before settling back in the bus where he eventually dies
  • Following his death McCandless' parents decide to visit the bus where he died
  • It had been ten months since he died when they visit, and they travel to the bus with Krakauer
  • At the bus a plaques is placed as a memorial for Chris McCandless
