Basic Info

  • Title
  • Into The Wild
  • Author
  • John Krakauer
  • Published
  • New York. Villard, 1996
  • Genre
  • Biography, Adventure
  • Setting
  • The novel follows Christopher McCandless' journey through the United States, Mexico, and Canada eventually leading to his death in Alaska. Throughout the novel various locations in the mid-west, west coast, and Alaska are mentioned in particular in which McCandless left his mark before entering the 'Wild'.
  • Point Of View
  • The novel is told in first and third person point of view by the author John Krakauer. Krakauer is in the first person as he describes his meetings with those who knew McCandless, and in the third person when detailing McCandless' journey.
  • Conflict
  • The major conflict depicted in the biography is between McCandless and his beliefs of society as well as his parents. McCandless struggles to cope with his parents marital issues, also he is dissatisfied with what he believes to be conformity everywhere especially in the college setting. He longs to set himself free and go on a life altering adventure. However his adventure brings even more conflicts, as he is not quite prepared for some of the challenges and succumbs to death in the Alaskan wilderness.
